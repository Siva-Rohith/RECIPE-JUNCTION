ALOO GOOBI

  • INGREDIENTS

  • Potatoes: 2 medium, peeled and diced
  • Cauliflower: 1 small head, cut into florets
  • Onion: 1 medium, finely chopped
  • Tomato: 1 medium, chopped (optional)
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece, grated
  • Garlic: 2-3 cloves, minced
  • Green chili: 1-2, slit (adjust to taste)
  • Turmeric powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Cumin seeds: 1 teaspoon
  • Coriander powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Garam masala: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Salt: to taste
  • Oil: 2 tablespoons (vegetable or mustard oil)
  • Fresh cilantro: for garnish

Optional

  • Peas: 1/2 cup (for added color and nutrition)
  • Lemon juice: for a tangy flavor

  • Preparation Process

  1. Prep the Vegetables:
    • Peel and dice the potatoes.
    • Cut the cauliflower into small florets.
    • Finely chop the onion and tomato (if using).
    • Grate the ginger and mince the garlic.
    • Slit the green chili.
  2. Heat the Oil:
    • In a large pan or skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium heat.
  3. Sauté the Spices:
    • Add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds to the hot oil. Let them sizzle for a few seconds until fragrant.
  4. Cook the Onions:
    • Add the finely chopped onion and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Add Ginger, Garlic, and Green Chili:
    • Stir in the grated ginger, minced garlic, and slit green chili. Cook for another minute until fragrant.
  6. Add Tomatoes (Optional):
    • If using, add the chopped tomato and cook until it softens.
  7. Add Spices:
    • Mix in 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of coriander powder, and salt to taste. Stir well.
  8. Add Vegetables:
    • Add the diced potatoes and cauliflower florets to the pan. Mix everything well to coat the vegetables with the spices.
  9. Cook Covered:
    • Add a splash of water (about 1/4 cup), cover the pan, and let it cook on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  10. Final Touches:
    • Once the vegetables are cooked, sprinkle 1/2 teaspoon of garam masala over the top. Stir gently.
  11. Garnish and Serve:
    • Remove from heat and garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve hot with roti, paratha, or rice.
