Samosa

  • INGREDIENTS

  • For the Dough:
  • All-purpose flour: 2 cups
  • Carom seeds (ajwain): 1 teaspoon
  • Salt: to taste
  • Oil: 2 tablespoons (for dough)
  • Water: as needed to knead the dough
  • For the Filling:
  • Potatoes: 3 medium, boiled and mashed
  • Peas: 1/2 cup (boiled)
  • Onion: 1 medium, finely chopped
  • Green chilies: 2, finely chopped
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece, grated
  • Coriander powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Cumin powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Turmeric powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Garam masala: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Salt: to taste
  • Oil: for frying

  • Preparation Process

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, carom seeds, and salt. Add oil and mix well.
  2. Gradually add water and knead to form a smooth and firm dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  3. For the filling, heat a little oil in a pan. Add onion and sauté until translucent.
  4. Add ginger, green chilies, and sauté for another minute.
  5. Mix in the boiled potatoes and peas. Add coriander powder, cumin powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook for a few minutes, then let it cool.
  6. Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each portion into a ball. Roll out into thin circles (about 6 inches in diameter).
  7. Cut each circle in half to form two semi-circles. Take one semi-circle, fold it into a cone shape, and seal the edges with a little water.
  8. Fill the cone with the potato filling and seal the open edge, ensuring there are no gaps.
  9. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Once hot, gently slide in the samosas and fry until golden brown and crispy.
  10. Remove and drain on paper towels. Serve hot with chutney or sauce.
