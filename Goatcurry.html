Goat Curry

  • INGREDIENTS

    • Goat meat: 500 grams, cut into pieces
    • Yogurt: 1/2 cup
    • Turmeric powder: 1/2 teaspoon
    • Red chili powder: 1 teaspoon
    • Coriander powder: 1 teaspoon
    • Garam masala: 1 teaspoon
    • Salt: to taste
    • Oil: 3 tablespoons
    • Onions: 2 large, finely sliced
    • Tomatoes: 2 medium, chopped
    • Ginger-garlic paste: 1 tablespoon
    • Green chili: 1-2, slit (optional, for spice)
    • Whole spices: (2 bay leaves, 4 green cardamoms, 4 cloves, 1 cinnamon stick)
    • Water: 2 cups (or as needed for desired consistency)
    • Fresh cilantro: for garnish

  • Preparation Process

  1. Marinate the Goat: In a bowl, combine goat meat with yogurt, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and marinate for at least 1 hour.
  2. Heat the Oil and Sauté Spices: In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat oil on medium heat. Add the whole spices (bay leaves, cardamoms, cloves, cinnamon stick) and sauté until they release aroma.
  3. Sauté Onions: Add sliced onions and cook until they turn golden brown. Then, add ginger-garlic paste and green chili, and sauté until the raw aroma disappears.
  4. Add Tomatoes and Cook: Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they are soft and oil begins to separate from the mixture.
  5. Add the Marinated Goat: Add the marinated goat meat to the pot and cook on medium-high heat for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally until the meat is browned and well coated with the spices.
  6. Simmer the Curry: Add water to the pot, cover with a lid, and let it cook on low heat for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the meat is tender. Stir occasionally and adjust water as needed for desired consistency.
  7. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with rice, naan, or roti.
