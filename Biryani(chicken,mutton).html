Biryani

  • INGREDIENTS

    • Meat (chicken, goat, or beef): 500 grams, cut into pieces
    • Basmati rice: 2 cups, soaked for 30 minutes
    • Yogurt: 1/2 cup
    • Ginger-garlic paste: 1 tablespoon
    • Red chili powder: 1 teaspoon
    • Turmeric powder: 1/2 teaspoon
    • Garam masala: 1 teaspoon
    • Lemon juice: 1 tablespoon
    • Fried onions (birista): 1/2 cup (for layering)
    • Mint leaves: 1/2 cup, chopped
    • Fresh cilantro: 1/2 cup, chopped
    • Saffron: a pinch, soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk
    • Whole spices: (1 bay leaf, 4 cardamoms, 1 cinnamon stick, 4 cloves, 1 star anise)
    • Salt: to taste
    • Ghee: 2 tablespoons
    • Oil: 2 tablespoons

  • Preparation Process

  1. Marinate the Meat: In a bowl, mix meat with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt. Marinate for at least 1 hour or overnight.
  2. Cook the Rice: In a large pot, boil water with the whole spices (bay leaf, cardamoms, cinnamon, cloves, star anise) and salt. Add the soaked basmati rice and cook until it's 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  3. Prepare the Meat: In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat oil and ghee. Saut√© marinated meat with fresh mint and cilantro. Cook until meat is browned and cooked through, and oil separates from the mixture. (For tougher meats like goat or beef, you may need to cook it further until tender.)
  4. Layer the Biryani: In the same pot, layer half of the partially cooked rice over the meat. Sprinkle with fried onions, mint, cilantro, and saffron milk. Repeat the layering with remaining rice and garnishes.
  5. Dum Cooking: Cover the pot with a tight lid. Cook on low heat for 25-30 minutes until rice and meat are fully cooked and flavors meld.
  6. Serve: Gently mix before serving, and enjoy with raita and salad.
