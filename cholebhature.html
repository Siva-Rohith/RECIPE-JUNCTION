Chole Bhature

  • INGREDIENTS

  • Chickpeas: 1 cup, soaked overnight
  • Water: as needed
  • Salt: to taste
  • Cumin seeds: 1 teaspoon
  • Onion: 1 large, finely chopped
  • Ginger-garlic paste: 1 tablespoon
  • Tomatoes: 2 medium, chopped
  • Turmeric powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Red chili powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Garam masala: 1 teaspoon
  • Coriander leaves: for garnish
  • All-purpose flour: 2 cups
  • Yogurt: 1/2 cup
  • Oil: for kneading and frying

  • Preparation Process

  1. For the chole, soak chickpeas overnight in water. Drain and rinse them.
  2. In a pressure cooker, add the soaked chickpeas, water, and a pinch of salt. Cook for about 3-4 whistles or until tender. Set aside.
  3. In a large pan, heat oil. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add onion and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  6. Add tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Cook until the tomatoes are soft.
  7. Mix in the cooked chickpeas along with some of the cooking liquid. Simmer for about 10-15 minutes to allow flavors to meld.
  8. Adjust seasoning with salt and garnish with coriander leaves.
  9. For the bhature, mix all-purpose flour, yogurt, oil, and a pinch of salt in a bowl. Knead into a soft dough.
  10. Cover the dough and let it rest for about 2 hours.
  11. Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each into a circle about 6 inches in diameter.
  12. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Fry each rolled dough in hot oil until puffed and golden brown on both sides.
  13. Serve the chole hot with the bhature, accompanied by sliced onions and lemon wedges.
