Hyderabadi Biryani

  • INGREDIENTS

    • Basmati rice: 2 cups, soaked for 30 minutes
    • Chicken: 500 grams, bone-in pieces (or use lamb or vegetables for vegetarian biryani)
    • Onions: 2 large, thinly sliced
    • Yogurt: 1/2 cup
    • Ginger-garlic paste: 1 tablespoon
    • Green chili: 2-3, slit (adjust to taste)
    • Mint leaves: 1/2 cup, chopped
    • Fresh cilantro: 1/2 cup, chopped
    • Lemon juice: 1 tablespoon
    • Fried onions (birista): 1/2 cup, for layering
    • Saffron: a pinch, soaked in 2 tablespoons of warm milk
    • Whole spices: (1 bay leaf, 4 green cardamoms, 1-inch cinnamon stick, 4 cloves, 1 star anise)
    • Turmeric powder: 1/2 teaspoon
    • Red chili powder: 1 teaspoon
    • Garam masala: 1 teaspoon
    • Salt: to taste
    • Ghee: 2 tablespoons
    • Oil: 2 tablespoons

  • Preparation Process

  1. Marinate the Chicken: Combine chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt. Set aside for at least 1 hour.
  2. Cook the Rice: Boil water in a large pot with whole spices (bay leaf, cardamoms, cinnamon, cloves, star anise) and salt. Add the soaked basmati rice and cook until it's 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  3. Prepare the Chicken or Meat: In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat oil and ghee. Fry sliced onions until golden. Add marinated chicken, fresh mint, and cilantro. Cook until chicken is almost done and oil separates.
  4. Layer the Biryani: In the same pot with the chicken, layer half of the partially cooked rice, sprinkle fried onions, mint, and saffron milk. Repeat layers with remaining rice and garnish ingredients.
  5. Dum Cooking: Cover the pot with a tight lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes until the rice and meat are fully cooked.
  6. Serve: Gently mix before serving, and enjoy with raita and salad.
